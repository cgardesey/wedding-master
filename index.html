We Love Each Other

Our Story

Sometimes we fall in love with the unexpected person at the most unexpected moments.

We used to be friends way back in Cape Coast; We were in the same congregration on campus. When we first met (2014-2016),we barely talked to each other and whenever we did, it was just for few minutes after congregation meetings. Sometimes, accidentally, we took pictures together after the memorial, assemblies or conventions.

Somewhere in 2017, we made arrangements to go out in service together every Friday late afternoon after lectures. During these times in the ministry, we talked at length and got to know each other well. We talked about almost everything; from family, spirituality,education, career, life experiences, hobbies, friends. She did most of the talking while I did the listening and asked questions where necessary.

Fast forward, Kobby completed and left Cape Coast to do his National Service in Accra but we still stayed in touch. Sometimes, he called to check on me to find out how I was coping at school.

Days turned to months, then came the first knock on her door but the response was “Give me some time to think about it”. So we continued being good friends and shared memories together both good and bad.

Things took an unexpected turn at some point when we both stopped contacting each other. One night I prayed to Jehovah, asking for a sign to help me choose a marriage partner since I wasn't getting any feedback from Genny. To my surprise, the sign pointed back to her. Since then, I became convinced in my heart that I had found my Love but was scared to ask the second time for fear of losing such a great friend.

Now we got in touch again and communicated more often, the more we spoke the more connected we became at hearts. We got to know each other on a personal level to the point of sharing some deep things. We became best friends: at any point in time, if there was anyone to turn to in any situation, we knew we had each other. The love, trust, and respect grew naturally.

On the 5th of October 2020, around 8pm, I finally mustered the courage to express my feelings to Genny again. That night was the beginning of many good things to come. Although separated about 190 km apart and with Covid-19 still lingering and lurking around, we still found ways to make our relationship work.

With just three days to our two years momentous relationship, I proposed at a very serene location she loved so much and she responded affirmatively. And here we are!

  • First Date as Friends

    March 6, 2020

    It was on the 6th of March 2020, at a favorite location in Cape Coast. We met there after almost 2 years of not seeing each other. We had a long talk and enjoyed each other's company.

  • First Date as a Couple

    May 2, 2021

    Our first date as a couple was 8 months after we officially started dating. It was during the covid-19 pandemic, that outing was indeed memorable because we got to see each other again in person and it was a triple date, with two couples with us. It was fun!!!

  • Second Date as a Couple

    May 6, 2021

    It was few hours after Genny wrote an exam in Accra and seeing grandpa off at the airport. That meeting was a stress reliever for both of us. We had good food and drinks, although it was short, we remember how soothing it was.

  • In a Relationship

    November 28, 2021

    Our journey together has been incredible. Two people from different backgrounds, having different interests and learning to adapt to each other, definitely has not been easy. But all that comes to mind are the beautiful moments because we choose love over everything and that has kept us in motion till now and to the years to come.

  • Engaged

    March 19, 2022

    Kobby proposed privately on 6th March 2022. We announced it publicly through the knocking ceremony which was done on the 19th of March 2022. Just 3 days to our 2nd anniversary as a couple, I was surprised with a stunning ring in a location I loved so much.
